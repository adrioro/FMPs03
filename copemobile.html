When We Lose a Pet
Losing a pet is a painful event in the life of a pet parent. In legal terms, pets are still considered properties. However, for most of us, pet owners, pets are a valuable part of our families. As a consequence, the trauma we experience with the loss of a pet is significant. Unfortunately, in many cases, we don't address the situation with the diligence required. Part of the reason is that the pain of the loss of a pet is not appreciated by everybody.  The concept that humans should not grieve the loss of a property, an item they can easily purchase in a shop, contradicts our feelings. Then, we find ourselves afraid to be perceived as overdramatic. As a consequence, we experience the pain alone. Grief becomes more difficult, extremely painful, and the call for help urgent. At this point, we should find alternative methods to cope with our feelings.
Create a place you can always come back to and revive your pet’s love!
Create Memorial
What Should We do to Cope?
1- Reflect on the wonderful times we shared with the pet we lost.

Take some time to reflect by doing activities that would help you cope with the pain. Narrate stories you will like to keep fresh in your memory. Write a poem to honor them. Make a portrait of your pet if you enjoy painting or drawing. Or create an online pet memorial here, at Forever My Pets, a place where you can always come back to remember those moments and share them with others. Use these activities to process your pain, but be positive and grateful for those moments you spent together over time. Nobody can’t take them away from you!

2- Give yourself time to grieve and process your emotions.

It is important to avoid stuffing our emotions. We do not want to find ourselves dealing with a more serious emotional state down the road. We might be busy, life might be demanding. But, designating small periods for us to feel and overcome the pain would be helpful. Suffering will not disappear by avoiding it, but it will transform and find its place if we deal with it. For that, we need time to grieve.


3- Your life continues and it is precious. Take care of it.

Grieving is difficult, but your life is important. Make an effort to continue meeting your daily needs. Eat well, avoid consuming food or drinks that would disturb your sleeping. Sleeping well might helps us to heal. Maintaining or improving our regular sleep schedule is crucial. Adopting a calming practice might help as well. We should take care of ourselves. After all, we are the special being that our pet so much loved. Let's honor their memory by honoring our lives.

Coping with Grief
